Here at Cal's Theta Zeta chapter of Delta Kappa Epsilon, we value ourselves as being men who embody all three of our fraternity's timeless qualities: the Gentleman, the Scholar and the Jolly Good Fellow. Each member of DKE prides himself on excelling in both academics and athletics. As a chapter, we host semesterly philatrophic events and brotherhood orientated trips.

THE FRATERNITY

Five Presidents of the United States have been alumni of DKE -- more than any other fraternity. They include Rutherford B. Hayes, Theodore Roosevelt, Gerald R. Ford, George H. W. Bush, and George W. Bush.

5

Delta Kappa Epsilon's illustrious list of alumni also includes the fourth person to walk on the moon, Alan Bean. The DKE flag has not only been flown on the moon, but was also planted on the first expedition to the North Pole.

4

Founded at Yale in 1844, DKE is one of the oldest North American fraternities with 68 active chapters across the United States and Canada.

68
OUR CHAPTER

Our chapter became the very first chapter of Delta Kappa Epsilon on the West Coast in 1876. Our house is located right across the street from campus as well as the Memorial Stadium, and includes an amazing view of the bay.

1

The Lion Trophy is the highest honor that can be acheived by a DKE chapter, and is awarded annually based on philanthropy, service, scholarship, and leadership. Our own Theta Zeta chapter has won it 5 times.

5

Our chapter boasts a 3.5 average GPA, and received the 2011 Scholarship Award from our national organization. With extensive test banks, and chapter alumni from Google to Goldman Sachs, DKE provides a valuable professional network.
